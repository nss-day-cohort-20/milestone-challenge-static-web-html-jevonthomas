About Me

Former Amazon Fulfillment Center Area Manager in Columbia, SC and an Army veteran with 5 years of active duty service as a Chemical Officer, with continued service in the Army Reserve as a Public Affairs Officer. A West Point graduate with a Bachelors Degree in Management and received an MBA from Western Governors University.

I did not enjoy the direction that my career path was headed, so I made the decision to enroll in Nashville Software School to make a career switch to Web Development and could not be happier about that decision.

I enrolled in Team Treehouse's Front End Web Development track, and used that to build on the foundations set by the two previously mentioned books. These courses reinforced what I had previously learned, while introducing me to newer concepts that I would dive deeper into in the future, such as Bootstrap and JQuery.

With a solid foundation set in Front End Web Development skills, I went on to Nashville Software School's Full Stack Developer Boot Camp in order to learn to design single page web applications. One truly useful aspect to this boot camp is its focus on training us in the ways that we will work once we graduate. We often worked in teams, utilizing Git as a source control method, and performed code reviews. These are skills that many self-taught developers enter the workforce lacking.
